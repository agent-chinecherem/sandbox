🔢 Mathematical Foundations

Music and mathematics are deeply intertwined. Every musical interval can be expressed as a frequency ratio, and these ratios determine whether we perceive harmony as consonant (pleasing) or dissonant (tense).

Interval in Cents = 1200 × log₂(f₂/f₁)
Perfect Ratios
Octave: 2:1 (1200 cents)
Perfect Fifth: 3:2 (702 cents)
Perfect Fourth: 4:3 (498 cents)
Complex Ratios
Major Third: 5:4 (386 cents)
Minor Third: 6:5 (316 cents)
Major Seventh: 15:8 (1088 cents)
Consonance Rule
Simpler ratios (smaller integers) tend to sound more consonant because their waveforms align more frequently, creating less beating.

🌍 Cultural Scale Systems

📚 Cultural Context

Western 12-TET
Equal temperament divides the octave into 12 equal semitones of 100 cents each. This allows transposition to any key but slightly compromises pure ratios.
Arabic Maqamat
Uses quarter-tones (50 cents) and neutral intervals. The Hijaz maqam features the distinctive augmented second interval.
Indian Ragas
Based on mathematical ratios but allows microtonal inflections. Different ragas evoke specific emotions and are associated with times of day.
Chinese Pentatonic
Built on perfect fifths (3:2 ratio). The five-note scale omits half-steps, creating a distinctive sound without sharp dissonances.
